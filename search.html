Sugar

Post by Jane Dane, Sep 27, 2019.
Food Ipsum

Sugar, primarily because of its high fructose content, affects metabolism in a way that sets us up for rapid fat gain and metabolic disease. Fructose gets metabolized by the liver and turned into fat which is secreted into the blood as VLDL particles. This leads to elevated triglycerides and cholesterol. It also causes resistance to the hormones insulin and leptin, which is a stepping stone towards obesity, metabolic syndrome and diabetes. Sugar causes a relentless biochemical drive for humans to eat more and get fat. (Healthline) Sugar is not bad if consumed in a balanced manner, but this not the case in the U.S. and in other developed countries.
